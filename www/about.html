The story so far

Jonathan Lambert is a successful entrepreneur, experienced consultant, published author and dynamic speaker with a proven track record of building and growing businesses across multiple industries.

Who I am

As a business catalyst, his objective is to enable individuals to connect with their deeper spiritual and personal selves and integrate these elements into their ventures, resulting in companies that not only drive positive impact and results, but also align with their personal values and sense of purpose.

Having amassed decades of experience as a CEO and investor in both San Francisco and New York City, he possesses a keen understanding of the critical role technology plays in driving business success. In today's digital-first, ai-enabled landscape, businesses that neglect to adopt a technology-first mindset and a deeper awareness of their roles and responsibilities risk falling behind or becoming irrelevant. As such, Jonathan firmly believes that every leader must be a "technology leader" simply to adequately harness the leverage provided by the latest digital tools and trends.

His expertise spans all aspects of companies, from early stage startups to enterprises. Over the years, he have worked with prominent companies and startups to create strategies and products that have reached billions of people, empowered executives and employees, and improved lives.

Open minds create futures.

Jonathan is an unshakeable optimist. He believes in a bright future and our ability to build it together

Described as "a visionary with a rare intellect,"" Jonathan has devoted his professional life to help advance a vision of the world that does not yet exist; a world in which the vast majority of people wake up every single morning inspired, safe, and fulfilled by the work they do.

Jonathan is a spark that ignites passion and ideas. His unconventional and innovative views on business and leadership have attracted international attention, and he has met with a broad array of leaders and organizations in nearly every industry. He frequently works with organizations and entrepreneurs around the world, and runs a venture incubator in addition to active roles in several companies to put his philosophy into action.

For 25+ years he has designed, created, and launched disruptive tech companies and lead mission-critical projects. Now he use his experience to help CEOs, boards of directors, and other leaders to transform with digital and AI.

Four keys

Take full responsibility

We cannot always control what happens, but we can control our reactions. Everything is easier when you take responsibility.

Power in the present

If you want to get cooking, you need to turn up the heat. And we only have power in this present moment: so do it now.

Embrace fear

Do one thing every day that scares you. There is only one thing that makes a dream impossible to achieve: the fear of failure.

In all things, balance

There is no one way. There is only your way. Finding your true purpose and expressing it is the greatest joy, besides giving.

I believe that the school system may not prioritize teaching us about money because they prioritize creating compliant employees rather than independent thinkers. Our education system teaches us everything in reverse. For instance, it focuses on perfectionism, individual testing, following instructions, avoiding technology, and accepting a single correct answer.

However, real life success is based on the opposite: embracing imperfection, leveraging technology, working collaboratively, and accepting that there are often multiple correct answers. Furthermore, the world is full of predatory individuals who seek to exploit others.

As a result, the school system instills a fear of making mistakes, discourages collaboration, and promotes an obsession with finding the "right" answer. It stifles a child's natural curiosity and passion for learning by demanding they sit still and be quiet.

As an entrepreneur and sometimes a mentor, a significant part of my work is to help people undo the damage caused by this outdated and harmful educational system. Unfortunately, only a small portion of the population receives an education that encourages creativity and independence, while most people are trained to be consumers and employees.

Therefore, give yourself a creator's education. Cultivate a creative mindset, create things, and pursue your interests with passion. Don't settle for a life of complacency or conformity.
